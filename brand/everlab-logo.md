# Everlab — Logo

---

## Logo Variants

### 1. Full Lockup (Primary)
The icon + wordmark together. This is the primary logo and should be used
in most contexts.

### 2. Wordmark Only
The "everlab" wordmark without the icon. Used for vertical applications or
where the icon is already established nearby.

### 3. Icon Only (The Mark)
The DNA helix / biomarker connector symbol on its own. Used at small sizes,
as a favicon, in video, or where the full lockup won't fit. It is the most
dynamic version — use it when you want flexibility.

---

## Colourways

The logo should only ever appear in **black or white**. No colour versions.

| Background | Logo colour |
|---|---|
| White / light backgrounds | Black logo |
| Black / dark backgrounds | White logo |
| Photography / busy backgrounds | White logo, or black with care |
| Crème / neutral backgrounds | Black logo |
| Orange / brand accent backgrounds | White logo |

---

## Minimum Sizes

Our logos have been carefully crafted to read well even at small sizes.
Always follow these minimums.

| Variant | Digital minimum | Print minimum |
|---|---|---|
| Full lockup | 34px height | 8mm height |
| Stacked / wordmark | 46px height | 8mm height |
| Icon only (the mark) | 30px height | 6mm height |

---

## Clear Space

Always ensure enough clear space around the logo. The minimum clear space
is equal to the height of the "e" in the wordmark on all sides. When in doubt,
opt for more clear space.

---

## Partner Lockup

When placing the Everlab logo next to a partner or collaborator logo:
- Use the full lockup (icon + wordmark)
- Separate with a thin vertical divider or generous spacing
- Both logos should be the same height
- Both logos should be the same colour (black or white)

---

## Misuse — What to Avoid

- Don't recolour the logo in any brand or custom colour
- Don't stretch or distort the proportions of the logo
- Don't rearrange the components of the logo
- Don't add drop shadows, outlines, or effects
- Don't place the logo on low-contrast backgrounds
- Don't animate the logo without brand team approval
- Don't recreate or redraw the logo — always use the approved files
